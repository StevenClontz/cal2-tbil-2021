Fact 4.9.8.

Here is a strategy checklist when dealing with series:

  1. The \(n^{th}\) term test: unless \(a_n\rightarrow 0\text{,}\) \(\displaystyle \sum a_n\) diverges

  2. Geometric Series: \(\displaystyle \sum ar^n\) converges if \(-1<r<1\) and diverges otherwise

  3. \(p\)-series: \(\displaystyle \sum \frac{1}{n^p}\) converges if \(p>1\) and diverges otherwise

  4. Series with no negative terms: try the ratio test, root test, integral test, or try to compare to a known series with the comparison test or limit comparison test

  5. Series with some negative terms: check for absolute convergence

  6. Alternating series: use the alternating series test (Leibniz's Theorem)

  7. Anything else: consider the sequence of partial sums, possibly rewriting the series in a different form, hope for the best
