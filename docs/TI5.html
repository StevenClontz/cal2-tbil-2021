Section 1.5 Tables of Integrals (TI5)

Learning Outcomes

  • I can integrate functions using a table of integrals.

Activity 1.5.1.

Consider the integral \(\int \sqrt{16-9x^2} \,dx\text{.}\) Which of the following substitutions appears most promising to find an antiderivaitve for this integral?

  1. \(\displaystyle u=16-9x^2\)

  2. \(\displaystyle u=9x^2\)

  3. \(\displaystyle u=3x\)

  4. \(\displaystyle u=x\)

  1. b.

Activity 1.5.3.

For each of the following integrals, identify which entry from Appendix A best matches the form of that integral.

  1. \(\displaystyle \int \frac{25x^2}{\sqrt{25x^2-9}} \,dx\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \int \frac{81x^2}{\sqrt{16-x^2}} \,dx\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{10x \sqrt{100-x^2}} \,dx\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \int \frac{7}{\sqrt{25x^2-9}} \,dx\)

  5. \(\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{25x^2+16}} \,dx\)

Example 1.5.4.

Here is how one might write out the explanation of how to find \(\int \frac{3}{x\sqrt{49x^2-4}} \,dx\) from start to finish:

\begin{align*} \int \frac{3}{x\sqrt{49x^2-4}} \,dx &&\text{Let }&u^2=49x^2\\ &&\text{Let }&a^2=4 \\ &&& u = 7x\\ &&& \,du = 7\,dx\\ &&& \frac{1}{7}\,du = \,dx\\ &&& a = 2\\ \int \frac{3}{x\sqrt{49x^2-4}} \,dx &= 3\int \frac{1}{x\sqrt{49x^2-4}} (\,dx)\\ &= 3\int \frac{1}{\frac{u}{7}\sqrt{u^2-a^2}} (\frac{1}{7}\,du)\\ &= 3\int \frac{1}{u\sqrt{u^2-a^2}} \,du & \text{which best matches f.}\\ &= 3(\frac{1}{a}\arcsec(\frac{u}{a}))+C\\ &= \frac{3}{2}\arcsec(\frac{7x}{2})+C \end{align*}
Activity 1.5.5.

Which step of the previous example do you think was the most important?

  1. Choosing \(u^2=49x^2\) and \(a^2=4\text{.}\)

  2. Finding \(u=7x\text{,}\) \(du=7\,dx\text{,}\) \(\frac{1}{7}\,du=\,dx\text{,}\) and \(a=2\text{.}\)

  3. Substituting \(\frac{3}{x\sqrt{49x^2-4}} \,dx\) with \(3\int \frac{1}{u\sqrt{u^2-a^2}} \,du\) and finding the best match of f from Appendix A.

  4. Integrating \(3\int \frac{1}{u\sqrt{u^2-a^2}} \,du=3(\frac{1}{a}\arcsec(\frac{u}{a}))+C\text{.}\)

  5. Unsubstituting \(3(\frac{1}{a}\arcsec(\frac{u}{a}))+C\) to get \(\frac{3}{2}\arcsec(\frac{7x}{2})+C\text{.}\)

Activity 1.5.6.

Consider the integral \(\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{64-9x^2}} \,dx\text{.}\) Suppose we proceed using Appendix A. We choose \(u^2=x^2\) and \(a^2=64\text{.}\)

  1. What is \(u\text{?}\)

  2. What is \(du\text{?}\)

  3. What is \(a\text{?}\)

  4. What do you get when plugging these pieces into the integral \(\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{64-9x^2}} \,dx\text{?}\)

  5. Is this a good substitution choice or a bad substitution choice?

Activity 1.5.7.

Consider the integral \(\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{64-9x^2}} \,dx\) once more. Suppose we still proceed using Appendix A. However, this time we choose \(u^2=9x^2\) and \(a^2=64\text{.}\) Do you prefer this choice of substitution or the choice we made in Activity 1.5.6?

  1. We prefer the substitution choice of \(u^2=x^2\) and \(a^2=64\text{.}\)

  2. We prefer the substitution choice of \(u^2=9x^2\) and \(a^2=64\text{.}\)

  3. We do not have a strong preference, since these substitution choices are of the same difficulty.
