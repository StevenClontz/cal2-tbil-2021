Section 4.5 Basic Convergence Tests (SQ5)

Learning Outcomes

  • Use the divergence, alternating series, and integral tests to determine if a series converges or diverges.

Activity 4.5.1.

For each of the following series, write out the first several terms, and then argue whether the series converges or diverges.

(a)

\(\displaystyle \sum_{n=0}^\infty n^2\)

(b)

\(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^\infty \displaystyle\frac{n+1}{n}\)

(c)

\(\displaystyle \sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n\)

Fact 4.5.2.

If the series \(\displaystyle\sum a_n\) is convergent, then \(\displaystyle\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} a_n=0\text{.}\)

Fact 4.5.3. The Divergence (\(n^{th}\) term) Test.

If the \(\displaystyle\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} a_n\neq 0\text{,}\) then \(\displaystyle\sum a_n\) diverges.

Fact 4.5.4.

If \(\displaystyle\sum a_n=A\text{,}\) \(\displaystyle\sum b_n=B\text{,}\) and \(c\) is a real number, then

  1. \(\displaystyle\sum ca_n=c\displaystyle\sum a_n=cA\text{,}\) and

  2. \(\displaystyle\sum a_n+\displaystyle\sum b_n=\displaystyle\sum (a_n+b_n)=A+B\text{.}\)

Fact 4.5.5.

If \(a_n>0\) for all \(n\text{,}\) then \(\displaystyle\sum a_n\) is convergent if and only if the sequence of partial sums is bounded from above.

Activity 4.5.6.

Consider the so-called harmonic series, \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^\infty \displaystyle\frac{1}{n}\text{,}\) and let \(S_n\) be its \(n^{th}\) partial sum.

(a)

Show that \(S_4\geq S_2+\displaystyle\frac{1}{2}\text{.}\)

(b)

Show that \(S_8\geq S_4+\displaystyle\frac{1}{2}\text{.}\)

(c)

Argue that the series converges or diverges.

Activity 4.5.7.

Consider the series \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^\infty \displaystyle\frac{1}{n^2}\text{.}\)

(a)

How does this series to \(\displaystyle\int_1^\infty \displaystyle\frac{1}{x^2} \, dx\text{?}\)

(b)

Draw a picture relating the sum and the integral (try comparing areas of appropriate regions in the plane).

(c)

Argue that the series converges or diverges.

Fact 4.5.8. The Integral Test.

Let \(\{a_n\}\) be a sequence of positive numbers. If \(f(x)\) is continuous, positive, and decreasing, and there is some positive integer \(N\) such that \(f(n)=a_n\) for all \(n\geq N\text{,}\) then \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=N}^\infty a_n\) and \(\displaystyle\int_N^\infty \displaystyle f(x) \, dx\) both converge or both diverge.

Fact 4.5.9. The \(p\)-Test.

The series \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^\infty \displaystyle\frac{1}{n^p}\) converges for _______, and diverges otherwise.

Activity 4.5.10.
(a)

What value of \(p\) corresponds to the harmonic series?

(b)

What do we know about the \(p\)-series with \(p=2\text{?}\)

(c)

Fill in the blank in the above fact, and then prove it.

Fact 4.5.12. The Alternating Series Test (Leibniz's Theorem).

The series \(\displaystyle\sum (-1)^{n+1}u_n\) converges if all of the following conditions are satisfied:

  1. \(u_n\) is always positive,

  2. there is an integer \(N\) such that \(u_n\geq u_{n+1}\) for all \(n\geq N\text{,}\) and

  3. \(\displaystyle\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}u_n=0\text{.}\)

Fact 4.5.13. The Alternating Series Estimation Theorem.

If the alternating series \(\displaystyle\sum a_n=\displaystyle\sum (-1)^{n+1}u_n\) converges to \(L\) and has \(n^{th}\) partial sum \(S_n\text{,}\) then for \(n\geq N\) (as in the alternating series test):

  1. \(|L-S_n|\) is less than \(|a_{n+1}|\text{,}\) and

  2. \((L-S_n)\) has the same sign as \(a_{n+1}\text{.}\)
