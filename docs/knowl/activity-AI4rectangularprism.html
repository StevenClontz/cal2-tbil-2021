Activity 2.2.1.

Consider a rectangular prism with a 10 m \(\times\) 10 m square base and height 20 m. Suppose the density of the material in the prism increases with height, following the function \(\delta(h)=10+h\) kg/m\(^3\text{,}\) where \(h\) is the height in meters.

(a)

If one were to cut this prism, parallel to the base, into 4 pieces with height 5 m, what would the volume of each piece be?

(b)

Consider the piece sitting on top the slice made at height \(h=5\text{.}\) Using \(\delta(5)=15\) kg/m\(^3\text{,}\) estimate the mass of this piece.

(c)

Is this estimate the actual mass of this piece?
