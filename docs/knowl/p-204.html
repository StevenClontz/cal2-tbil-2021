Suppose we let \(x=\tan(\theta), dx =\sec^2(\theta)\text{.}\) Which of the following best represents the integral \(\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{1+x^2} dx\text{?}\)

  1. \(\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{1+\tan^2(\theta)} dx\text{.}\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{1+\tan^2(\theta)\sec^2(\theta)} d\theta\text{.}\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{1+\tan^2(\theta)} \sec^2(\theta)d\theta\text{.}\)
