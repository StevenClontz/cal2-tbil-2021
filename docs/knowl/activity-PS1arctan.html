Activity 4.1.18.

Recall that

\begin{equation*} g(x)=\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^\infty x^n=\frac{1}{1-x} \end{equation*}

for \(-1< x< 1\) and \(\frac{d}{dx}[\arctan(x)]=\frac{1}{1+x^2}=g(-x^2).\)

(a)

Find the power series for \(g(-x^2)\text{.}\)

(b)

Anti differentiate this power series and find \(C\) to find a power series for \(H(x)=\arctan(x)\text{.}\)

(c)

Use the 6th degree polynomial approximation for \(\arctan(x)\text{,}\) \(H_6(x)\) to estimate \(\arctan(4)\) by computing \(H_6(4)\text{.}\)
