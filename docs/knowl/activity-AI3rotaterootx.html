Activity 2.2.6.

Consider the function \(f(x)=\sqrt{x}\) and the solid of revolution generated by rotating the region bound by:

\begin{align*} 0&\leq y \leq f(x)\\ 0& \leq x \leq 4 \end{align*}

about the \(x\)-axis.

We wish to find the surface area of this solid.

(a)

What is the area of the circular “base” of the solid at \(x=0\text{?}\)

  1. \(\pi\cdot 0^2\text{.}\)

  2. \(\pi\cdot 2^2\text{.}\)

  3. \(\pi\cdot 4^2\text{.}\)

  4. \(\pi\cdot x^2\text{.}\)

  5. \(\pi\cdot (\sqrt{x})^2\text{.}\)

(b)

What is the area of the circular “base” of the solid at \(x=4\text{?}\)

  1. \(\pi\cdot 0^2\text{.}\)

  2. \(\pi\cdot 2^2\text{.}\)

  3. \(\pi\cdot 4^2\text{.}\)

  4. \(\pi\cdot x^2\text{.}\)

  5. \(\pi\cdot (\sqrt{x})^2\text{.}\)

(c)

We now need to find the surface area of the “side”.

Suppose we estimate the surface area of this solid by approximating it with two cylinders of “height” \(\Delta x=2\) and radii \(f(0)=0, f(2)=\sqrt{2}\text{:}\)

What is the area of the “side” of the cylinder with radius \(f(0)=0\text{?}\)

  1. \(2 \pi\cdot 0\cdot 2\text{.}\)

  2. \(2 \pi\cdot 2\cdot 2\text{.}\)

  3. \(2 \pi\cdot \sqrt{2}\cdot 2\text{.}\)

  4. \(2 \pi\cdot 4\cdot 2\text{.}\)

(d)

What is the area of the “side” of the cylinder with radius \(f(2)=\sqrt{2}\text{?}\)

  1. \(2 \pi\cdot 0\cdot 2\text{.}\)

  2. \(2 \pi\cdot 2\cdot 2\text{.}\)

  3. \(2 \pi\cdot \sqrt{2}\cdot 2\text{.}\)

  4. \(2 \pi\cdot 4\cdot 2\text{.}\)

(e)

Suppose we now use a “height” of \(\Delta x=1\text{:}\)

Fill out the following table:

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|} \hline x_i & \Delta x & f(x_i) & \text{Estimated "Side" Area}\\ \hline x_1=0 & 1 & & \\ \hline x_2=1 & 1 & & \\ \hline x_3=2 & 1 & & \\ \hline x_4=3 & 1 & & \\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}
(f)

Which of the following Reimann sums best approximates the “side” area of our solid?

  1. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \sum 2\pi x_i\Delta x\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \sum 2\pi (\sqrt{x_i})\Delta x\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \sum \pi x_i^2\Delta x\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \sum \pi (\sqrt{x_i})^2\Delta x\)

(g)

Which of the following integrals computes the “side” area of our solid?

  1. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \int_0^4 2\pi xdx\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \int_0^4 2\pi (\sqrt{x})dx\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \int_0^4 \pi x^2dx\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \int_0^4 \pi (\sqrt{x})^2dx\)
