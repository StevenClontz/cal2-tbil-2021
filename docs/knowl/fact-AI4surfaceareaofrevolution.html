Fact 2.2.11.

Consider a function \(f(x)\) and the solid of revolution generated by rotating the area bound by:

\begin{align*} 0&\leq y \leq f(x)\\ a& \leq x \leq b \end{align*}

about the \(x\)-axis. The surface area of this solid is approximated by the sum

\begin{equation*} SA\approx \sum_{i=1}^n 2\pi f(x_i)\Delta x+\pi\left(f(a)\right)^2+\pi\left(f(b)\right)^2 \end{equation*}

and computed by the integral

\begin{equation*} SA=\lim_{n\to\infty}\left( \sum_{i=1}^n 2\pi f(x_i)\Delta x+\pi\left(f(a)\right)^2+\pi\left(f(b)\right)^2\right)=\int_a^b 2\pi f(x)dx+\pi\left(f(a)\right)^2+\pi\left(f(b)\right)^2 \end{equation*}
