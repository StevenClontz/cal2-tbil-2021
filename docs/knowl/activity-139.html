Activity 2.3.14.

Suppose we wanted to find the volume of the the solid of revolution generated by rotating the region in the first quadrant bound by the curves:

\begin{equation*} x=0, x=3, y=0, y=\frac{4}{2^x} \end{equation*}

about the \(y\)-axis.

(a)

Suppose we wanted to estimate this volume with 3 concentric annular cylinders with heights \(h_i=f(r_i)=\frac{4}{2^{r_i}}\text{,}\) where \(r_i\) are the inner radii and \(R_i\) are the outer radii. Over which values do the \(r_i, R_i\) range?

  1. \([-3,3]\text{.}\)

  2. \([0,3]\text{.}\)

  3. \([-4,4]\text{.}\)

  4. \([0,4]\text{.}\)

(b)

Recall that the volume of an annular cylinder of height \(h\text{,}\) inner radius \(r\) and outer radius \(R\) is \(V=\pi(R^2-r^2)h\text{.}\)

Fill out the following table:

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|} \hline r_i & \Delta r_i & ; R_i=r_i+\Delta r & h_i=f(r_i)=\frac{4}{2^{r_i}} & \text{Estimated Volume}\\ \hline r_1=0 & 1 & R_1=0+1=1 & & \\ \hline r_2=1 & 1 & R_2=1+1=2 & & \\ \hline r_3=2 & 1 & R_3=2+1=3 & & \\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}
(c)

Consider an arbitrary annular cylinder with inner radius \(r_i\text{,}\) outer radius \(r_i+\Delta r\text{,}\) and height \(h_i=\frac{4}{2^{r_i}}\text{.}\)

Which of the following represents the volume of this annular cylinder?

  1. \(\pi r_i^2\cdot \frac{4}{2^{r_i}}\text{.}\)

  2. \(\pi (r_i+\Delta r)^2\cdot \frac{4}{2^{r_i+\Delta r}}\text{.}\)

  3. \(\pi ((r_i+\Delta r)^2-r_i^2)\cdot \frac{4}{2^{r_i}}\text{.}\)

  4. \(\pi ((r_i+\Delta r)-r_i)^2\cdot \frac{4}{2^{r_i}}\text{.}\)

(d)

Recall that \(\pi ((r_i+\Delta r)^2-r_i^2)=\pi(2(\Delta r) r_i+(\Delta r)^2)=\pi(2 r_i+(\Delta r))\Delta r\text{.}\) Which of the following Riemann sums best estimates the volume of our solid?

  1. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \sum \pi(2 r_i+(\Delta r))\frac{4}{2^{r_i}}\Delta r\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \sum \pi r_i^2\frac{4}{2^{r_i}}\Delta r\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \sum \pi (r_i+\Delta r)^2\frac{4}{2^{r_i}}\Delta r\)

(e)

Which of the following integrals computes the volume of our solid?

  1. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \int_{-3}^3 2\pi r \frac{4}{2^r} dr\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \int_{0}^3 2\pi r \frac{4}{2^r} dr\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \int_{0}^3 2\pi r \frac{4}{2^r} dr\)

(f)

What is the volume of our solid?
