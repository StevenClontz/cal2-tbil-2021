What can we say about the series

\begin{equation*} \displaystyle 5+\frac{5}{2}+\frac{5}{3}+\frac{5}{4}+\cdots+\frac{5}{n}+\cdots? \end{equation*}

  1. \(\displaystyle \frac{5}{2}+\frac{5}{3}+\frac{5}{4}+\cdots+\frac{5}{n}+\cdots\) converges to a known value we can compute.

  2. \(\displaystyle \frac{5}{2}+\frac{5}{3}+\frac{5}{4}+\cdots+\frac{5}{n}+\cdots\) converges to some unknown value.

  3. \(\displaystyle \frac{5}{2}+\frac{5}{3}+\frac{5}{4}+\cdots+\frac{5}{n}+\cdots\) diverges.
