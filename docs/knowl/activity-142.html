Activity 2.4.3.

Suppose we wanted to find the surface area of the the solid of revolution generated by rotating

\begin{equation*} y=\sqrt{x}, 0\leq x\leq 4 \end{equation*}

about the \(y\)-axis.

(a)

Suppose we wanted to estimate the surface area with two fulstrums with \(\Delta x=2\text{.}\)

What is the surface area of the fulstrum formed by rotating the line segment from \((0,0)\) to \((2, \sqrt{2})\) about the \(x\)-axis?

  1. \(2\pi \frac{0+\sqrt{2}}{2}\cdot2\text{.}\)

  2. \(2\pi \frac{0+\sqrt{2}}{2}\cdot\sqrt{2^2+\sqrt{2}^2}\text{.}\)

  3. \(\pi \sqrt{2}^2\cdot2\text{.}\)

  4. \(\pi \sqrt{2}^2\cdot\sqrt{2^2+\sqrt{2}^2}\text{.}\)

(b)

What is the surface area of the fulstrum formed by rotating the line segment from \((2,\sqrt{2})\) to \((4, 2\) about the \(x\)-axis?

  1. \(2\pi \frac{4+\sqrt{2}}{2}\cdot\sqrt{2}\text{.}\)

  2. \(2\pi \frac{4+\sqrt{2}}{2}\cdot\sqrt{6}\text{.}\)

  3. \(2\pi \frac{4+\sqrt{2}}{2}\cdot\sqrt{6-2\sqrt{2}}\text{.}\)

(c)

Suppose we wanted to estimate the surface area with \(n\) fulstrums.

Let \(f(x)=\sqrt{x}\text{.}\) Which of the following expressions represents the surface area generated bo rotating the line segment from \((x_0, f(x_0))\) to \((\Delta x, f(x_0+\Delta x))\) about the \(x\)-axis?

  1. \(\displaystyle \pi \left(\frac{f(x_0)+f(x_0+\Delta x)}{2}\right)^2\sqrt{(\Delta x)^2+(f(x_0+\Delta x)-f(x_0))^2}\text{.}\)
