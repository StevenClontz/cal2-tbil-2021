Activity 3.3.2.

Recall that

\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{4}+\frac{1}{8}+\cdots+\frac{1}{2^n}+\cdots=1. \end{equation*}
(a)

What can we say about the series

\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \frac{3}{2}+\frac{3}{4}+\frac{3}{8}+\cdots+\frac{3}{2^n}+\cdots? \end{equation*}

  1. \(\displaystyle \frac{3}{2}+\frac{3}{4}+\frac{3}{8}+\cdots+\frac{3}{2^n}+\cdots\) converges to \(3\cdot 2=6\text{.}\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \frac{3}{2}+\frac{3}{4}+\frac{3}{8}+\cdots+\frac{3}{2^n}+\cdots\) converges to some value other than 6.

  3. \(\displaystyle \frac{3}{2}+\frac{3}{4}+\frac{3}{8}+\cdots+\frac{3}{2^n}+\cdots\) diverges.

(b)

What can we say about the series

\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \frac{3.1}{2}+\frac{3.01}{4}+\frac{3.001}{8}+\cdots+\frac{3+(0.1)^n}{2^n}+\cdots? \end{equation*}

  1. \(\displaystyle \frac{3.1}{2}+\frac{3.01}{4}+\frac{3.001}{8}+\cdots+\frac{3+(0.1)^n}{2^n}+\cdots\) converges to \(3\cdot 2=6\text{.}\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \frac{3.1}{2}+\frac{3.01}{4}+\frac{3.001}{8}+\cdots+\frac{3+(0.1)^n}{2^n}+\cdots\) converges to some value other than 6.

  3. \(\displaystyle \frac{3.1}{2}+\frac{3.01}{4}+\frac{3.001}{8}+\cdots+\frac{3+(0.1)^n}{2^n}+\cdots\) diverges.
