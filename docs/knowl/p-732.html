How might we best utilize Fact 4.6.11 to determine the convergence of \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n\) or \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=0}^\infty b_n\text{?}\)

  1. Since \(a_n\) is sometimes greater than, and sometimes less than \(b_n\text{,}\) there is no way to utilize Fact 4.6.11.

  2. Since initially, we have \(b_n\leq a_n\text{,}\) we can utilize Fact 4.6.11 by assuming \(a_n\geq b_n\text{.}\)

  3. Since we can rewrite \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n=\sum_{n=0}^{99} a_n+\sum_{n=100}^\infty a_n\) and \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=0}^\infty b_n=\sum_{n=0}^{99} b_n+\sum_{n=100}^\infty b_n\) and \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=0}^{99} a_n, \sum_{n=0}^{99} b_n\) are necessarily finite, we can compare \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=100}^\infty a_n, \sum_{n=100}^\infty b_n\) with Fact 4.6.11.
