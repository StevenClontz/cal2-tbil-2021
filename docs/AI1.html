Section 2.1 Average Value (AI1)

Learning Outcomes

  • Compute the average value of a function on an interval.

Activity 2.1.1.

Suppose a car drives due east at 70 mph for 2 hours, and then slows down to 40 mph for an additional hour.

(a)

How far did the car travel in these 3 hours?

(b)

What was their average veclocity over these 3 hours?

Activity 2.1.2.

Suppose a car drives due east at a velocity of \(v(t)=40+\sqrt{300t}\) mph, where \(t\) is the number of hours after they start driving.

(a)

How far did the car travel in these 3 hours?

(b)

What was their average veclocity over these 3 hours?

Activity 2.1.3.

Which of the following is the average value of \(f(x)\) over the interval \([0,8]\text{?}\) Note \(f(x)=\begin{cases} 1, & 0\leq x\leq 3 \\ 4, & 3 < x \leq 6 \\ 3, & 6 < x \leq 8 \end{cases}\text{.}\)

  1. \(4\text{.}\)

  2. \(2\text{.}\)

  3. \(\frac{7}{3}\text{.}\)

  4. \(2.375\text{.}\)

Activity 2.1.4.

Consider a function \(f(x)\) which has average value \(v\) on the interval \([a,b]\text{.}\) Which of the following is most likely always true about \(v\text{?}\)

  1. \(v\) is the average of \(a\) and \(b\text{.}\)

  2. \(v\) is the average of \(f(a)\) and \(f(b)\text{.}\)

  3. \(v\) is the average of \(\min\{f(x)\}\) and \(\max\{f(x)\}\) on the interval \([a,b]\text{.}\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \int_a^b f(x)dx=\int_a^b vdx\text{.}\)

Definition 2.1.5.

Given a function \(f(x)\) defined on \([a,b]\text{,}\) it's average value \(v\) is:

\begin{equation*} v=\frac{1}{b-a}\int_a^b f(x) dx. \end{equation*}

Activity 2.1.6.

Which of the following expressions represent the average value of \(f(x)=x\cos(x^2)+x\) on the interval \([\pi, 4\pi]\text{?}\)

  1. \(\displaystyle \int_{0}^{4\pi}x\cos(x^2)+x\text{.}\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \frac{1}{3\pi}\int_{0}^{4\pi}x\cos(x^2)+x\text{.}\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \frac{1}{4\pi}\int_{0}^{4\pi}x\cos(x^2)+x\text{.}\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \int_{\pi}^{4\pi}x\cos(x^2)+x\text{.}\)

  5. \(\displaystyle \frac{1}{3\pi}\int_{\pi}^{4\pi}x\cos(x^2)+x\text{.}\)

  6. \(\displaystyle \frac{1}{4\pi}\int_{\pi}^{4\pi}x\cos(x^2)+x\text{.}\)
