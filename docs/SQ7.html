Section 4.7 Ratio and Root Tests (SQ7)

Learning Outcomes

  • Use the ratio and root tests to determine if a series converges or diverges.

Fact 4.7.1.

Let \(N\) be an integer and let \(\displaystyle\sum a_n\) be a series with \(a_n\geq 0\) for \(n\geq N\text{,}\) and suppose that \(\displaystyle\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\sqrt[n]{a_n}=\rho\text{.}\) Then

  1. \(\displaystyle\sum a_n\) converges if \(\rho\) is less than 1, and

  2. \(\displaystyle\sum a_n\) diverges if \(\rho\) is greater than 1.

Fact 4.7.2.

Let \(\displaystyle\sum a_n\) be a series with positive terms, and suppose that \(\displaystyle\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n}=\rho\text{.}\) Then

  1. \(\displaystyle\sum a_n\) converges if \(\rho\) is less than 1, and

  2. \(\displaystyle\sum a_n\) diverges if \(\rho\) is greater than 1.
