Section 4.8 Absolute Convergence (SQ8)

Learning Outcomes

  • Determine if a series converges absolutely or conditionally.

Activity 4.8.1.

Consider the series: \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^n}{n}\text{.}\)

(a)

Does the series \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^n}{n}\) converge or diverge? (Recall Fact 4.5.12.)

(b)

Does the series \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^\infty \left|\frac{(-1)^n}{n}\right|\) converge or diverge? (Recall Activity 4.5.6.)

Definition 4.8.2.

Given a series

\begin{equation*} \sum a_n \end{equation*}

we say that \(\displaystyle \sum a_n\) is absolutely convergent if \(\displaystyle \sum |a_n|\) converges.

Activity 4.8.3.

Consider the following series:

  1. \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{n^2(-1)^n}{n^3+1}\text{.}\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{n^2}\text{.}\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^n \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^n\text{.}\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^\infty -n\text{.}\)

(a)

For each of the series, determine if the series converges using any method.

(b)

For each of the series, compute \(|a_n|\text{.}\)

(c)

For each of the series, determine if the series is absolutely convergent.

Activity 4.8.4.

If you know a series \(\displaystyle \sum a_n\) is absolutely convergent, what can you conclude about whether or not \(\displaystyle \sum a_n\) is convergent?

  1. We cannot determine if \(\displaystyle \sum a_n\) is convergent.

  2. \(\displaystyle \sum a_n\) is convergent since it “grows slower” than \(\displaystyle \sum |a_n|\) (and falls slower than \(\displaystyle \sum -|a_n|\)).

Fact 4.8.5.

If \(\displaystyle \sum a_n\) is absolutely convergent, then it must be convergent.

Activity 4.8.6.

Find 3 series that are convergent but not absolutely convergent (recall Fact 4.5.12, Section 4.6).
